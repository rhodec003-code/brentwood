Helping Seniors with Hoarding

Heavy clutter in a senior’s home requires patience, privacy, and a process that protects dignity while improving safety and function.

Families often feel overwhelmed when a parent or loved one is living with rooms full of papers, clothing, furniture, collections, or years of unopened boxes. The first step is to slow the project down enough to create trust. Rushing decisions can increase anxiety and make the work harder.

Brentwood Organizers approaches senior hoarding and heavy clutter from the organizing and coordination side. That can include sorting, planning, donation coordination, document review, photo preservation, and preparing the home for safer daily use.

A respectful process

  • Start with the safest and most-used rooms first.
  • Create simple categories: keep, donate, discard, shred, family review, and undecided.
  • Protect important paperwork, photographs, medication information, and valuables.
  • Coordinate donation pickup, disposal, shredding, and specialty vendors when needed.

When additional help is needed

If the home has biohazards, pests, mold, structural concerns, or clinical hoarding disorder needs, qualified specialty providers may be required. An organizer can still help the family understand the project, coordinate next steps, and preserve important belongings where appropriate.

The goal is not perfection. The goal is a safer home, clearer pathways, calmer routines, and a plan the senior and family can live with.
