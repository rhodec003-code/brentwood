Client Testimonial

Organizing is so much more than organizing. It is about helping people feel seen, supported, and cared for during times of transition.

Today, one of my clients asked me to join him on a walk. We couldn’t make it work, so we rescheduled — and it stayed with me. He’s in his 70s, his family lives in Europe, and we bonded after I organized his home and shared stories about our rescued pets.

Moments like these remind me why I love what I do.

Earlier this month, I helped another client, “Lisa,” after her cousin passed away. A hospice worker gave her my information. Lisa was the only living relative, lived out of state, and had just undergone back surgery. Her request was simple: pack the valuable items, ship them to her, and clear out the rest of the house.

She was surprised to learn the hospital and funeral home did not handle personal matters. I stepped in to take care of everything and offer peace of mind during an emotional time.

These are the experiences that make this work worthwhile. Organizing is not just about creating order. It is about showing up during transition, building relationships, and making life easier.

If you’re a realtor or work with seniors, you’ve seen how overwhelming these situations can be. Whether helping a senior downsize, supporting someone after loss, or staging a home for sale, I bring care, compassion, and the ability to say yes when it matters most.

Organizing is so much more than organizing — it’s about helping people feel seen, supported, and cared for.
