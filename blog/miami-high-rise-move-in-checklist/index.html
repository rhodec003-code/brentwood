Miami High-Rise Move-In Checklist

Moving into a Miami high-rise requires COIs, freight-elevator reservations, loading-dock windows, and strict building timelines.

Moving into a Miami high-rise is not like moving into a single-family home. Most condo and HOA buildings require Certificates of Insurance, freight-elevator reservations, loading-dock windows, and strict time limits, often with deposits and paperwork.

30–45 days before move-day

  • Ask for your building’s move-in packet.
  • Book the freight elevator and loading dock.
  • Confirm truck access and garage clearance.
  • Line up your movers and COIs.

7–10 days before

  • Submit COIs and forms to management.
  • Confirm your elevator time window.
  • Share your floor plan with movers and organizers.
  • Label boxes by room and contents on at least two sides.
  • Use “First-Open” labels for bedding, coffee, chargers, and essentials.

Move-day

Check in with security with printed COIs and reservation confirmations. Use pads, runners, and corner guards as required. Stage boxes by zone at entry, then move to rooms in label order.

After move-in

Unpack essentials first: beds, bath, daily-use kitchen, and chargers. Break down boxes and schedule disposal per building rules. Photograph any unit or common-area damage and notify management promptly.

How we help

We coordinate COIs and reservations, box mapping, floor-plan planning, and full unpack and home setup in 1–2 days.

FAQs

Do I always need a COI?

If you’re moving into a condo or high-rise, assume yes.

How far ahead should I reserve the elevator?

Policies vary from 48 hours to 2 weeks. Book as early as possible.
