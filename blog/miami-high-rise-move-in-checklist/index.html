Move-In Checklist

Miami High-Rise Move-In Checklist

High-rise moves require more coordination than a standard home move. Building access, elevator windows, delivery timing, and storage planning all matter.

Before move day

  • Confirm building move-in rules, certificates of insurance, parking, elevator reservations, and loading dock access.
  • Measure closets, pantry shelves, storage cages, and laundry areas.
  • Label boxes by room and priority, not only by category.
  • Decide what should be unpacked first: kitchen basics, bath essentials, linens, wardrobe, office, and daily technology.

After the movers leave

The first days should focus on function. Set up the kitchen, primary closet, bathrooms, bedding, and daily supplies before decorative or low-priority items. A professional move-in organizer can direct placement, edit duplicates, and build systems as items come out of boxes.

For Brickell, Miami Beach, Bal Harbour, and downtown Miami condos, a clear move-in plan prevents weeks of living around half-opened boxes.
