As a professional organizer, I don't personally sell items or handle any transactional services. However, I work closely with a trusted auctioneer who can take over when it comes to evaluating and selling valuable pieces.

Auctioneers are licensed professionals, which means they're held to specific legal, ethical, and financial standards that vary by state. They must be bonded, insured, and trained in proper valuation, fair bidding practices, and handling of client property.

As a professional organizer, I recommend working with a licensed auctioneer because it offers greater protection, transparency, and peace of mind—especially when selling high-value or sentimental items. Estate sales, while popular, aren't always regulated and can sometimes lack oversight or fair market pricing.
